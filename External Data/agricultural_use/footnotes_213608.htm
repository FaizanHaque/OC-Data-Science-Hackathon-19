How was the data set created?

Derivation of measure

Measures 1 & 2: These measures were derived from the 2001, 2006, and 2011 Multi-Resolution Land Characteristics (MRLC) consortium National Land Cover Database (https://www.mrlc.gov/finddata.php). The NLCD land cover estimates are a grid of 30m x 30m pixels, each categorized as one of 20 land cover categories meeting different criteria of interest. For each state, county and census tract, the percentage of the area covered by developed land (open space, low, medium or high intensity) and agriculture (i.e., pasture/hay or cultivated crops), individually, were calculated using GIS software.

Measures 3 & 4: National Center for Health Statistics (NCHS) data systems are often used to study the associations between urbanization level of residence and health and to monitor the health of urban and rural residents. NCHS has developed a six-level urban-rural classification scheme for U.S. counties and county-equivalent entities. The most urban category consists of "central" counties of large metropolitan areas and the most rural category consists of nonmetropolitan "noncore" counties.(https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/data_access/urban_rural.htm).

What are the limitations of the data set?

Measures 1 & 2:

MRLC National Land Cover Database (NLCD):

  • NLCD data are updated every 5 years, with some lag for when the update is released, so the data do not necessarily represent current conditions on the pixel-scale.
  • NLCD products are intended for use in identifying regional landscape patterns and major functional habitats.
  • Small features and changes should be verified with a higher resolution data source.

Measures 3 & 4:

NCHS Urban-Rural Classification Scheme for Counties:

  • The NCHS Urban-Rural Classification Scheme for Counties should only be used with data files where all counties are identified.
  • Changes in the classification of counties that result when urbanization schemes are updated ensure that current settlement patterns are represented, but they can make comparison of statistics computed using different versions of the scheme problematic.
  • Review 2013 NCHS Urban-Rural Classification Scheme for Counties and the 2006 and 1990 NCHS Urban-Rural Classification Scheme for Counties for information regarding any additional limitations and conclusions for the NCHS Urban-Rural Classification data.
